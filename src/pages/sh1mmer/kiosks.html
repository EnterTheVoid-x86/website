What you'll need

  • A currently un-enrolled Chromebook
  • A brain; note that it has to be competent alongside being there.

    • Enabling Developer Mode

    Press the power button (⏻), reload key (↻), and ESC key at the same time to enter the recovery screen. It'll look like one of the images below:

    On this screen, press CTRL + D at the same time, then press enter to enable developer mode.

    After around six minutes of loading dev mode, press CTRL + D at the same time again to get past of the developer screen and onto ChromeOS. The screen should look like one of the images below:

    After pressing CTRL + D on the previous screen, you should now be loading into ChromeOS with developer mode!

    Powerwashing

    If you just enabled developer mode, your Chromebook should already be powerwashed, so you won't need to proceed with this step.

    If you already had developer mode enabled and have data, you will need to powerwash the Chromebook again.

    Sign out of your account, then press CTRL + ALT + SHIFT + R at the same time on the account selection screen, or log in screen. It should look like the image below:

    From here, press "Powerwash" and proceed with the on-screen instructions and let it finish.

    Re-Enrollment

    Go through the setup process and you should end up at this screen below:

    Press the "Enterprise Enrollment" button and log in with your school account to re-enroll into the Chromebook.
